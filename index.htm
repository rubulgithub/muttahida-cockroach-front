Why This Movement Exists

The Three
Betrayals.

We were betrayed by our opponents who demonised us. We were betrayed by our so-called protectors who used us. We were betrayed by our own leaders who sold us. This movement starts by naming all three, loudly.

🗳️
The Vote Bank Trap
Every election cycle, "secular" parties arrive with fear, biryani, and promises. Every five years, we deliver votes. Every six years, we wait. Masjids get funded. Colleges don't. Madrasas get subsidies. Skill centres don't. The deal is not development — it is dependence. The Sachar Committee documented it in 2006. Nothing changed.
🤝
Our Own Political Gundas
Muslim leaders who speak for us in parliament have bungalows in Lutyens while our youth have no jobs. Maulanas who endorse parties get nothing in writing. Local dons who deliver Muslim votes like cattle pocket the reward. The exploitation isn't only external. Our community deserves elected representatives accountable to outcomes, not identity performance.
📜
Rights on Paper Only
The Indian Constitution guarantees equal citizenship, freedom of religion, and due process. These rights exist. Their enforcement does not. Political prisoners rot under UAPA. Bulldozers demolish without court orders. NRC threatens citizenship based on paperwork poverty can't fix. The law says one thing. The state does another. We demand alignment.
The Manifesto

Eight Demands.
No Compromise.

These are not appeals. These are constitutional rights being demanded by Indian citizens who pay taxes, vote, fight wars, win medals, and build this nation — and are owed this by law.

01
Political Prisoners
Release Muslims Jailed Under UAPA Without Trial
Art. 21 & 22

Hundreds of Indian Muslims — students, journalists, activists — are imprisoned under UAPA for years without bail, charge sheet, or trial. We demand: time-bound trials within 90 days, immediate bail review for undertrial prisoners jailed beyond 2 years, and an independent audit of all UAPA cases filed after 2019. No Indian should be a political prisoner.

02
Citizenship Rights
NRC & CAA Must Not Create Second-Class Citizens
Art. 14 & 15

The CAA-NRC combination is constitutionally dangerous. It makes religion a criterion for citizenship for the first time in independent India. We demand: complete rollback of NRC, judicial review of CAA's religious discrimination, and a guarantee that no Indian Muslim — regardless of documentation gaps caused by poverty or displacement — shall be rendered stateless.

03
Voting Rights
Protect Every Muslim Vote — Deletions Are Disenfranchisement
Art. 326

Systematic deletion of Muslim names from voter rolls is documented across multiple states. This is the silent undoing of democracy. We demand: any Election Commissioner found complicit in voter list manipulation faces prosecution under UAPA — as the Cockroach Janta Party demands. Taking away a vote is no less than electoral terrorism. We stand by that framing.

04
Anti-Bulldozer
Demolitions Without Due Process Are Illegal — Full Stop
Art. 21

"Bulldozer justice" — demolishing homes of accused individuals disproportionately in Muslim neighbourhoods — is extrajudicial punishment violating the right to life and Supreme Court precedent. We demand: mandatory court orders before any demolition, immediate rehabilitation for those whose properties were illegally razed, and criminal prosecution of officials who ordered unconstitutional demolitions.

05
Education & Economy
Empowerment, Not Appeasement — Education Is the Demand
Art. 21A & 29

Muslim India has one of the lowest literacy rates, lowest government employment, and lowest access to formal banking — confirmed by the Sachar Committee in 2006, unchanged in 2026. We demand: Muslim representation in IITs, IIMs, and central universities proportional to population; skill development centres in Muslim-majority districts; Aligarh Muslim University's minority status restored; and an audit of Waqf properties to fund education, not politics.

06
Anti-Gundagiri
Expose & Remove Muslim Political Dons Who Exploit the Community

We name the problem: Muslim politicians across parties who monetise community fear, deliver votes as a commodity, incite communal tension for relevance, and suppress genuine civic voices in exchange for personal benefit. We demand: internal audits of Muslim legislators' work, public pledges on deliverables signed before every election, and a social boycott of leaders who have held office for 10+ years without measurable outcomes.

07
Communal Justice
End Impunity for Communal Violence Instigators
Art. 14

From Muzaffarnagar to Jahangirpuri — perpetrators of mob violence and political hate speech walk free while victims are jailed. We demand a Special Fast Track Court mechanism exclusively for communal violence cases, with mandatory prosecution of instigators regardless of political affiliation. Equal protection under law is not a privilege. It is a constitutional guarantee.

08
No More Vote Bank
Indian Muslims Will Vote on Development — Not Fear

We formally declare: this community will no longer vote as a bloc out of fear of the other side. Every party that wants Muslim votes must publish a written development manifesto for Muslim constituencies — on education, employment, housing, and legal protection. We will publish a community scorecard before every election. No deliverables. No votes. Full stop.
